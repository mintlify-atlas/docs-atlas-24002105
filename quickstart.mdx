## Get started with Gemini prompts

This guide walks you through everything you need to start using prompts from this collection with Google Gemini.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Gemini">
    Go to [https://gemini.google.com/app](https://gemini.google.com/app) and sign in with your Google account.

    Gemini (formerly Bard) is Google's AI assistant. It accepts natural language prompts and generates text responses — from short answers to long-form content, code, plans, and more.

    <Tip>
      You can use Gemini directly in your browser without installing anything. For Google Workspace users, Gemini is also available inside Docs, Gmail, Sheets, and other apps.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Understand the basics of prompt structure">
    A good prompt gives Gemini enough context to produce a useful response. Before diving into the prompt library, familiarize yourself with the core best practices:

    - **Use natural language** — write prompts as you would explain a task to a person
    - **Be clear and concise** — avoid vague instructions; state what you want directly
    - **Provide context** — include relevant background so Gemini can tailor its response
    - **Use specific and relevant keywords** — precise language produces more accurate output
    - **Break down complex tasks** — split multi-part requests into separate prompts for better results

    For a deeper guide, see [Prompt best practices](/guides/best-practices).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Use your first prompt">
    Try this example from the official Copilot prompts in the collection:

    ```text
    Create a 12-week study plan for learning a new language: Italian
    ```

    Paste this into Gemini and press Enter. Gemini will generate a structured week-by-week plan covering vocabulary, grammar, listening, and speaking practice.

    <Tip>
      Experiment by swapping "Italian" for any other language, or adding context like your current skill level or available study time per day. More context leads to more tailored output.
    </Tip>

    Here are a few more prompts from the collection to try:

    ```text
    write a medium-style blog post about [topic]
    ```

    ```text
    Give me three fantasy short story ideas that include a magical object and a reluctant hero.
    ```

    ```text
    Draft a short, friendly email introducing myself as a freelance [your expertise]. Include these points: [list your key skills]. End with a question to encourage a response.
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Explore the prompt library">
    The collection includes prompts organized by use case — from everyday productivity and writing to advanced reasoning frameworks and specialized role prompts.

    Browse the full library at [Prompt library](/prompts/overview) to find prompts for your specific needs.

    <Tip>
      If you find a prompt that works well, adapt it. The best prompts are ones you've tuned to your own context and goals.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Using Gemini in Chinese

If you want Gemini to respond in Simplified Chinese — and avoid garbled or mixed-language output — add the following phrase to your prompt:

```text
使用简体中文回答，符合简体中文的表达习惯
```

This instructs Gemini to respond in Simplified Chinese in a way that matches natural Chinese expression habits. Add it at the end of any prompt when you want a Chinese-language response.

<Note>
  This tip comes directly from the source repository. It is especially useful when working with prompts that were originally written in English but need a Chinese-language output.
</Note>
