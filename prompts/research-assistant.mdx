Gemini can act as a structured research assistant: you provide the options and criteria, and it returns a recommendation with supporting analysis. This is especially useful when comparing products, services, or technical tools where you want both a clear recommendation and a balanced view of trade-offs.

## The core research pattern

The most effective research prompts share a common structure:

1. **List the items** you want to compare
2. **Specify your priorities** — what matters most to you
3. **Request structured output** — a recommendation, explanation, and comparison table

This pattern forces Gemini to produce a decision, not just a summary.

## Example prompt

The following prompt from the Awesome Gemini Prompts collection illustrates this pattern:

```text Product comparison
I'm considering these phones: [list models]. Prioritizing camera, speed, and battery life, which would you recommend? Provide a short explanation of your choice, and create a pros and cons table at the end.
```

Replace `[list models]` with specific phone models, for example: `iPhone 16 Pro, Google Pixel 9 Pro, Samsung Galaxy S25 Ultra`.

## Variations of the pattern

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Product comparison">
    ```text Comparing physical products
    I'm considering these phones: [list models]. Prioritizing camera, speed, and battery life, which would you recommend? Provide a short explanation of your choice, and create a pros and cons table at the end.
    ```

    Use this when comparing consumer hardware, appliances, or any physical product with measurable specs.
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Service comparison">
    ```text Comparing services
    I'm evaluating these project management tools: [list services]. My priorities are ease of use, integrations with Slack and GitHub, and pricing for a team of 10. Which would you recommend? Provide a short explanation and a pros and cons table.
    ```

    Adapt this for SaaS tools, cloud providers, agencies, or any service where fit-to-requirements matters.
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Technical tool comparison">
    ```text Comparing technical tools
    I need to choose a frontend framework for a new web app: [list frameworks]. I'm prioritizing developer experience, performance, and ecosystem maturity. Which would you recommend for a team with React experience? Include a pros and cons table.
    ```

    Use this when evaluating libraries, frameworks, databases, or infrastructure options.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Tips for better research results

<Tip>
  Be specific about ranking criteria. Instead of "good performance," say "sub-200ms response times for a read-heavy API." Specific criteria produce specific, useful comparisons.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  Ask for structured output explicitly. Requesting a table, bullet points, or a numbered list ensures the response is easy to scan and share.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  Always request pros **and** cons. Asking only for positives produces biased output. Explicitly requesting both sides gives you a more honest analysis.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  Use follow-up prompts to go deeper. After getting an initial recommendation, ask: `What are the most common complaints users have about [recommended option]?` or `Under what circumstances would [alternative option] be the better choice?`
</Tip>

<Note>
  Gemini's knowledge has a training cutoff. For pricing, availability, or recent product releases, verify the output against current sources before making purchasing decisions.
</Note>
